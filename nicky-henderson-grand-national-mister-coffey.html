Nicky Henderson prays agonising 44-year wait for Grand National winner will end with horse 'absolute

August 2024 · 4 minute read

NICKY HENDERSON is praying his heartbreaking 44-year wait for a Grand National winner will end thanks to a horse 'absolutely made for' Aintree.

The Seven Barrows supremo looks to have an impressively strong hand heading into next month's Cheltenham Festival.

Massive Champion Hurdle fav Constitution Hill spearheads a squad including Mares' Novice Hurdle fav Luccia, Arkle fancy Jonbon and rejuvenated Ryanair leading chance Shishkin.

Mister Coffey will contest the National Hunt Chase that week - but far greater riches could come in April.

The eight-year-old gelding is owned by Lady Bamford - the wife of JCB billionaire Lord Bamford.

Mister Coffey was bought for £340,000 in May 2019.

The £500,000 first-place prize in the Grand National would more than pay off that fee - and end Hendo's hoodoo in the world's most famous race.
