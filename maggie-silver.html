A Brief Bio About Adam Silver

Adam Silver is an American businessman, lawyer, film producer, and commissioner of the NBA.

He was born into a Jewish-American family on April 25, 1962, to his father, Edward Silver, in Rye, New York. As of 2023, he is 61.

His mother’s information is unavailable at the moment, while his father was a labor law lawyer and a senior partner at Proskauer Rose.

Adam joined Rye High School and graduated in 1980. After that, he went to Duke University and studied political science.

In 1984, he graduated with Bachelors’s degree in Arts and worked as a legal assistant at Les AuCoin for a few years.

He graduated in 1988 with a J.D. degree from University of Chicago Law School.

Then he served as a clerk for Judge Kimba Wood of the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York.

Later, he worked as an associate in Cravath, Swaine & Moor law firm.

He joined the NBA in 1992 and has worked in various positions within the league.

He worked under his predecessor and mentor, David Stern, as a chief operating officer and deputy commissioner in 2006.

Following Stern’s retirement in 2014, he became the new commissioner. 

Silver is known for being the top dawg of the NBA because he promoted the league into a massive global franchise, especially in China.

He has also maintained social justice and equality in the league.

In 2014, Silver was a trending name in the news for forcing Los Angeles Clippers owner, Donald Sterling, to sell the club after he made some racist remarks.

Moreover, Adam banned Sterling from the league for life.
