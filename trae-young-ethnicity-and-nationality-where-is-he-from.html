Trae Young Ethnicity And Nationality: Where Is He From?

September 2024 · 3 minute read

Atlanta Hawks’ Trae Young’s popularity has also raised curiosity about his ethnicity and personal life among his fans. Young is a biracial player.

After making NBA history on Thursday as the third-youngest player to hit 900 career three-pointers during the Atlanta Hawks vs. Orlando Magic game, Trae Young has garnered a lot of public attention.

Read to know more about Trae Young, his ethnicity, and his personal life.

Trae Young is an American professional basketball player currently for the Atlanta Hawks of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

Trae started his basketball journey at Norman North High School. He later joined the University of Oklahoma, showcasing his exceptional skills. There, he led the NCAA in both points and assists during a historic season.

On June 21, 2018, his hometown team, the Dallas Mavericks, drafted Young in the NBA draft. However, he was quickly traded to the Hawks.

Young’s rookie year earned him a unanimous selection to the NBA All-Rookie First Team. He continued to impress, securing two NBA All-Star appearances in his early career.

The 2020-21 season marked a turning point as Young led the Hawks to the Eastern Conference Finals. Later, in 2021, he signed a five-year max extension, emphasizing his pivotal role in the team.

Beyond the NBA, Young contributed to the U.S. men’s national U18 team, winning gold in the 2016 FIBA Americas Under-18 Championship.

Trae Young Ethnicity And Nationality

Born on September 19, 1998, in Lubbock, Texas, to proud parents Candice and Rayford Young, Trae grew up in Norman, Oklahoma, alongside his three siblings Timothy, Caitlyn, and Camryn.

He is an American by nationality. Talking about his ethnic background, Trae Young has a biracial ethnicity.

Trae Young has a biracial ethnicity, with African-American and Caucasian roots. His father, Rayford Young, is of African-American descent, while his mother, Candice Young, is of Caucasian descent.

This biracial background reflects Young’s heritage’s diversity, contributing to his identity’s rich tapestry.

In addition to his athletic prowess, Young is a proud Christian, drawing inspiration from his mother, Candice, who is a pastor’s daughter.

The values instilled in him through his faith have been a guiding force in navigating the challenges and triumphs of his career.

Meet Trae Young’s Family

Trae’s parents, dad Ray Young and mom Candice Young support his career. His parents were in high school and continued to date at Texas Tech before getting married and having Young.

Young actually comes from a family that has a history with basketball. He was raised by his mom and grandparents back as his dad played overseas and couldn’t be in the States most of the time.

His father, Ray, played college basketball at Texas Tech University and later played professionally in Europe. Rayford has always been beside Trae, supporting his journey.

On one particular occasion where Trae was the most-hated man on the court during the Hawk’s game against the Knicks, Ray said, “Even though he’s in the NBA right now, he’s an All-Star, he’s a Top 5 pick, it still feels like he has to prove himself”.

“Which, for me, as a dad, sometimes I feel unfortunate for him, and I just tell him you don’t have to prove anything to anybody.”
