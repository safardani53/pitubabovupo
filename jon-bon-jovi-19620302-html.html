John Francis Bongiovi Jr., better known as Jon Bon Jovi, is an American singer-songwriter, record producer, philanthropist, and actor. He is best known as the founder and conductor of Grammy-winning rock band Bon Jovi, founded in 1983. He has released 15 studio albums with his band as well as two solo albums. In the 1990s, Bon Jovi began his acting career, starring in films Moonlight and Valentino and U-571 and appearing on television in Sex and the City, Ally McBeal and The West Wing. As a musician, Bon Jovi was honored in the Writers Hall of Fame in 2009. In 2012, he ranked 50th on Billboard Magazine's "Power 100" list, a "right the most influential and powerful force in the music world ”. . In 1996, People magazine named him one of the "50 most beautiful people in the world". In 2000, People gave him the title "Sexiest rock star".
