GWEN Stefani has given fans a glimpse at her 1,300-acre Oklahoma ranch, showing off an enormous field full of flowers.

The former No Doubt frontwoman and her husband Blake Shelton share the ranch, which is situated near Tishomingo, Oklahoma.

Gwen, 53, took to her TikTok account to show off an idyllic field full of blooming flowers.

The field is located outside of her Oklahoma ranch, which sits on a property that spans more than 1,300 acres.

The musician captioned the post: "Oklahoma summers."

Gwen and Blake, 47, built the mansion before they tied the knot, according to The New York Post.

In addition to the flower field, there are wooded areas on the property and it is situated near a large lake.

The Voice judge has been showing off snippets of her Oklahoma residence on social media while enjoying her time there with her hubby.

Earlier this month she gave her followers a tour of her flower garden on the ranch.

She filmed a pink hibiscus plant full of blooms as well as other flower arrangements she put together.

Gwen showed off a vase full of yellow sunflowers and another full of pink lilacs and yellow and orange zinnias.

FLOWER FARMERS

Last month, she shared a similar video showcasing a field of sunflowers.

Gwen recorded as she walked behind Blake next to the field.

In the video, she said, "There he goes. Hardest working guy I've ever met. Making me work too!" as she pointed the camera at the country singer.

Then, she moved the camera to the field of flowers and said, "We did that! With God's help."

Later, she revealed that she clipped some of her flowers to display in her home.

They arranged the flowers in vases to display in a vase on what appeared to be a dining table. 

In the background of one of the clips, there was a large metal peacock positioned in the corner of their dining room.

She also shared a video of a bucket full of fresh vegetables, grown on the property.

WORKING HARD

A few days prior to that post, Blake starred in a TikTok video that showed fans a different side of his and Gwen's ranch.

Taking the camera to give a 360 view, he showed off the massive ranch while making up his own jingle.

He sang: "Take the truck and cut up this tree."
