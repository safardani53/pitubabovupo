Grassy field where Prince Harry lost virginity to older woman revealed

April 2024 · 3 minute read

THIS is the “grassy field” where Prince Harry lost his virginity to a horse-loving older woman — behind the aptly named pub the Rattlebone Inn.

Sources close to the Duke of Sussex at the time confirmed the location to The Sun after Harry detailed the event in his memoir Spare.

He described his al fresco romp in Sherston, Wilts, as a “quick ride, after which she smacked my rump and sent me off to graze”.

The pub’s landlord, who was not in charge at the time, said locals had told him how Harry and his brother William were frequent visitors to the 16th century boozer.

Our source said: “Both William and Harry had some wild nights in that pub and it’s definitely where the incident happened.

“I think she was mid-20s and he was still at school.”

The pub, located on the village high street, is an eight-minute drive from their father Charles’s Highgrove estate.

Its outdoor area features a model of a bare bottom in a thong which has been fixed to the wall — although this is not thought to be a reference to Harry.

In 2002 there were allegations the Duke, then aged 17, had engaged in a raucous drinking session and smoked cannabis in the pub’s outdoor area and had “lock-ins”.

There were even reports at the time that, during one drunken episode, Harry called the French chef a “f**king Frog” when he asked him to leave the pub.
