April 2024 · 4 minute read

ALDI customers are begging for their favorite summer products to return to stores this season.

Shoppers know and love Aldi for the affordable prices on high quality items, but occasionally, some popular products get discontinued.

As the summer months ramp up, loyal Aldi customers are remembering their favorite seasonal items of yesteryear and holding out hope that they could return.

Items get discontinued for various reasons.

Sometimes, supply chain challenges or customer complaints will get a product booted.

Most of the time, however, products disappear due to low sales.

Whatever the reason, customers hope these products will make a comeback very soon.

The U.S. Sun compiled a full list of Aldi shoppers’ favorite discontinued summer items.

1. S’MORES JUMBO COOKIE KIT

Summer is the time for roasting marshmallows over a fire and chowing down on a delicious S’mores treat.

Previously, Aldi took full advantage of the summer season, regularly selling a ginormous S’mores Cookie Kit from the brand Baker’s Corner.

Most read in Money

The kit came stocked with a cookie crust, chocolate, marshmallows and even a pan to easily serve those at your summer party.

A full cookie cake served 16 people and was priced just $5.

When the kit first launched, shoppers were “seriously impressed,” according to one Reddit review.

“So pleased with how the S’mores Jumbo Cookie Kit tastes!” one Redditor shared of the Aldi treat. “And more marshmallows than expected.”

However, the sweet treat didn’t stay around long.

Despite being well-liked by Aldi shoppers, it only lasted two years on shelves.

In late June of 2020, the ginormous cookie kit was only around for seven days before being totally sold out and never restocked.

While the S’mores Cookie Kit is sorely missed by Aldi shoppers, you can still find the retailer selling different cookie kits – for instance, the Mother’s Day Giant Cookie Kit also is available for just a few dollars in May.

2. COCKTAIL-INSPIRED SUMMER CHEESES

Nothing makes a summer party more delicious than a charcuterie board filled with all your favorite cheeses.

Aldi made charcuterie board season even more exciting by adding a boozy addition to its cheese lineup a few years back.

The store’s cocktail-inspired summer cheeses came courtesy of its Emporium collection.

Featuring Pina Colada, Peach Bellini and Espresso Martini flavors, the collection offered something truly different compared to most cheeses on the market.

The cocktail-flavored cheeses only stuck around for one summer, but those who remember the cheeses are hoping this year might be time for the cheeses to make a major return.

3. VISTA BAY MARGARITA HARD SELTZERS

For many, hard seltzers are the unofficial drink of the summer.

While Aldi still offers a vast array of wine and beer, it’s been missing a key product in the seltzer department these days.

As the ready-to-drink canned cocktail industry grows and moves into hard seltzer’s market, Aldi decided to get rid of the Vista Bay Margarita Hard Seltzers this year.

While White Claw and Truly remain an easy alternative to the Vista Bay beverages, many don’t feel they can replace the Margarita drink.

4. SPICY NACHO CHEESE BURGERS

Summer is also the time for cookouts and barbeques.

For those looking for a more unique flavor in their burgers, the Mae’s Kitchen Spicy Nacho Cheese Burgers were a solid choice.

The vegetarian burger was made with beans, vegetables and cheese with an added nacho spice to boost the flavor profile.

Shoppers adored the item until it mysteriously vanished in 2020, prompting complaints from customers across the country.

"All of our products are under constant review... and changes are made for various reasons,” Aldi said in response.

However, it never offered a full explanation for the product discontinuation or indicated if the item could make a return one day.

5. CHOCOLATE PEANUT BUTTER ICE CREAM

Aldi shoppers have been in a "deep despair" since the store discontinued its Specially Selected Chocolate Peanut Butter Ice Cream.

The retailer discontinued the dessert last month, and shoppers haven't been the same since.

"If Aldi gets rid of the chocolate peanut butter ice cream … I will fall into a despair so deep I will likely never recover," one shopper said of the fallen product on Twitter.

The item was a creamy chocolate peanut butter concoction that regularly had customers eating far more than the single serving recommendation.
