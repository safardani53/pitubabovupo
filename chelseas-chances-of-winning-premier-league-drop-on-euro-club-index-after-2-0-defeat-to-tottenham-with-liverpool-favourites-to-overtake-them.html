Chelsea's chances of winning Premier League drop on Euro Club Index... with Liverpool favourites to

August 2024 · 2 minute read

CHELSEA'S defeat to Tottenham last night has dealt a huge blow to their title aspirations.

That's according to the Euro Club Index, and while the Blues are clear favourites, the White Hart Lane reversal has given the other title chasers renewed hope.

Prior to the Spurs game Chelsea had a 66.7 per cent chance of winning the Premier League, according to the Index.

Fast forward to their first defeat in 14 games courtesy of a brace for the much-talked about Dele Alli, and the title race has perhaps been blown open.

This led to their chances of winning the title falling to 58.4 per cent.

They are still predicted to finish seven points clear of Liverpool in second and Manchester City in third.

The Index has Antonio Conte's side ending the season on 84 points, with Liverpool and Manchester City only separated by goal difference on 77 points apiece.

Despite their stunning win, Spurs are still predicted to finish behind their old rivals Arsenal in fifth, with Manchester United taking the sixth place.

Hull, Sunderland and Swansea are the three teams tipped to be relegated, the Tigers finishing rock bottom on 30 points, four from safety.

On Wednesday night Antonio Conte’s top of the table side were looking to equal Arsenal’s Premier League record of 14 consecutive wins, but a pair of headers from England star Alli sealed a memorable win for Spurs to lift them to third.

It was a miserable night all-around for the Blues, with team-mates Diego Costa and Pedro involved in a heated-exchange early in the first half.
