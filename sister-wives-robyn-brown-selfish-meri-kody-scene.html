Sister Wives fans rip 'selfish' Robyn Brown as she sobs and begs Meri to stay in relationship with K

January 2024 · 3 minute read

SISTER Wives fans have slammed Robyn Brown for begging Meri to stay in a relationship with Kody.

Viewers blasted the "selfish" star, 45, for sobbing to Meri, 52, and trying to convince her to stay with their husband Kody, 54.

In a teaser from Sunday's episode of Sister Wives, Meri and Kody agreed to finally end their relationship, leaving Robyn in tears.

At the start of the scene, Meri told Kody: "I know you don't feel or consider yourself married to me."

Robyn whispered, "That's not true," to Meri as Kody sighed in frustration.

He pointed out that he had agreed to stay married to someone only if he was in love with them.

The Brown family patriarch said: "If I'm ever not in love with you I won't just sit there and be in a pathetic place with you."

Robyn said tearfully, "That doesn't mean the marriage is over," referring to Meri and Kody's marriage.

Meri said: "It's not going to change what our relationship has been for years. Nothing is going to change."

Robyn said shakily to Meri: "I was hanging on with you."

Someone else raged: "This freaking fake crying Robyn. Didn’t Meri say several times how they hadn’t talked in months or robin didn’t invite her over for Easter or someone’s visit?

"The way she’s acting you’d think they were joined at the hip!!! She is the definition of a hypocrite!!!!"

IT'S OVER

Meri and Kody sparked split rumors as early as 2020, when Meri posted cryptic quotes on social media and ditched her wedding ring.

On January 10, 2023, Meri shared an official joint statement with Kody about their divorce saying: "After more than a decade of working on our relationship in our own unique ways, we have made the decision to permanently terminate our marriage relationship."

The statement continued: "During this process, we are committed to kindness and respect toward each other and to all members of our family, and we are also committed to the continued healing of any and all relationships within the family so that we can move forward with forgiveness, grace, and love."

"We started a breakup seven years ago and have been going through just a process of trying to figure out what to do," Kody told Us Weekly.
