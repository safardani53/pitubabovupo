What are microfibre towels made of?

Microfibre towels are made of a blend of polyester and polyamide - both fibres are extremely soft, stretchy and don't crease.

Working together, the two fibres help the product's durability - so it lasts a long time, even when washed over and over again.

It also has some serious strength and is resistant to abrasion.

Are microfibre towels good for the beach?

Microfibre towels are excellent for the beach and arguably even more suitable than conventional cotton towels as they're lighter, they dry quicker and many can even repel send.

Because they're ultra-lightweight and fold down really compact, microfibre towels will squeeze into tight beach bags with ease and their feather-light feel means you'll hardly even notice them.

Are microfibre towels good for hair?

Yes, definitely. If you suffer from frizz and flyaways, you should be using a microfibre towel. Microfibre towels don't create friction with your hair like normal towels, and absorb water faster - without leaving your hair too dry.

They're way more gentle on your hair than your usual towels, and don't leave any traces of lint or fluff.

How do you clean microfibre beach towels?

You can bung your microfibre towel into the wash with your usual laundry - as long as you don't use fabric softener. Fabric softener clogs the towel's "pores" and renders the fabric useless.

Be sure not to wash the microfibre towel with anything fluffy as lint will transfer onto it.
