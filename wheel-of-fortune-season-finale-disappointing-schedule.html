Wheel of Fortune fans disappointed as season finale ends with no big winner and 'accidentally

April 2024 · 4 minute read

WHEEL of Fortune's Season 40 has spun to a close without a big winner, but with an NSFW-looking puzzle.

At one point during the last non-rerun episode until September, the board looked quite suggestive to internet users.

Wheel of Fortune aired its final spin of its XL Season 40 on Friday.

According to WheelofFortunehistory.com, tonight's episode marked the season finale and the game show will not return until September.

With that, Pat Sajak, 76, introduced the season's last contestants.

There was Ariel, who “sings around the house” with his wife, father of four Johnny, and Tammy, married to her “handsome” former marine husband.

Unfortunately, one puzzle during the triple toss-up puzzles started appearing in a curious fashion that fans were quick to pick up on.

The puzzle read as P__CH__NG _ __NT at one point and it was eventually solved as “Pitching a tent”

Yet, Twitter fans wrote: “I mean, I know it's not "punching a c*nt".... But is it tho?”

“We love "inappropriate solves" from Wheel Of Fortune. My wife and I had the exact same one at the same time” wrote another.

Ariel wound up getting the bonus puzzle as "Checking My Phone Again winning a Mercedez Benz - this brought him to $84,000.

“I'd call that a pretty good night!” Pat said.

As the credits rolled, Pat nor Vanna acknowledged things were winding down.

In fact, Pat said: "We'll see you back here for more Wheel of Fortune on Monday" which seemed odd since it was the season finale, and seemed to be advertising reruns.

'MORE LIKE XS SEASON!'

Fans were a little bummed the season didn't end with a bang, or anyone winning the $1M wedge - but there were a few $100,000 winners over its eight-plus months, at least.

One fan wrote under a YouTube clip: “Thank goodness this is the final episode for this season because considering this is their XL season, the bonus round category options for the winning contestants was serving XS.

“Hopefully, next season and for future seasons, we would see a lot less of Thing and Phrase popping up as options for the bonus round.

Another wrote: “No million-dollar winners this 40th season. That's it, everybody. We have a shutout.

A third wrote: No million dollar winners due to unforeseen circumstances... Sorry, everyone. We'll try again next year for season 41."

And a fourth poured one out: “This is it, guys. The final episode of the 40th Season of Wheel of Fortune this year.

The last $1M wedge was won on October 17, 2021 on Celebrity Wheel of Fortune by none other than Melissa Joan Hart who guessed BRAN MUFFINS, for what it's worth.

NEW SPIN?

Meanwhile, Sony recently commenced early discussions about a kid-focused version of the popular game show, according to The Hollywood Reporter.

The spinoff might incorporate a few kid-friendly twists into the established gameplay.

Contestants might participate in physical and comedic obstacles in order to earn letters or buy vowels.

Joe D’Ambrosia, executive VP and general manager of SPT — Kids, spoke to the publication about the spinoff plans for WOF and Shark Tank.

"Our mission is straightforward and clear," he said.

“We’d like to be known as a premier content creator for kids who build new global franchises for Sony like the SPT scripted team has done with Cobra Kai, the game show division has accomplished with Jeopardy! and the international team with The Crown, but in the kids’ space.”

It is unclear if longtime WOF co-hosts Pat Sajak, 76, and Vanna White, 66, are involved in the upcoming spinoff.

REMEMBERING SEASON 40!

There were many new twists from the long-running game show during Season 40 that have impressed the die-hards, all said.

There was a rare Friday Finals during WWE week (meaning the three highest scorers during that special went to a finals episode).

And Wheel is now sometimes airing when a Final Spin isn't so final and winds up needing to be spun more than once- instead of editing out if a contestant messes up on the so-called final spin.

In addition, the game show aired a "fan favorites" week which included the return of the contestant Pat tried to "wrestle."
